Nabih.

Finding the minutes a student stopped listening.

From the heartbeat on their wrist, then writing those minutes back as a short lesson. The heart answers to the autonomic nervous system: it can't be performed like a face, and it doesn't interrupt like a survey.

Pipeline
01 Sensing A wearable records the heartbeat across the lecture
02 Features Beats located, gaps measured, HRV over 60-second windows
03 Affect A Random Forest classifies each window's emotional state
04 Attention Emotional state becomes an engaged or disengaged call
05 Lesson An LLM writes the missed minutes back into a short lesson
On the name

n-b-h, an Arabic root that holds both halves of this system at once: intibāh, the attention it measures, and tanbīh, the alert it sends back.

Nabih is the adjective from that root: alert, quick to notice, the one who catches what others miss.

One word for the input and the output.
